# Part IV — Package

*The third move: assemble bound, active context into a product an AI agent can reason over.*

Parts II and III made data trustworthy and its context active. But a governed table is not yet something an agent can reason over — it needs meaning *packaged*: an ontology, a semantic layer, a knowledge graph, and usage intelligence, exposed through an API. Part IV builds that, continuing the Meridian case study and opening on the "Adeyemi trust" question the copilot cannot answer well because the answer lives in *meaning*, not data. Part III hardened and activated the context; Part IV is where the domain crosses **Level 2 → 3**, as the ontology completes Modelled and the knowledge graph plus the live copilot make it Connected.

**The arc.** Chapter 15 draws the line between a *data product* (trustworthy data, meaning left to the consumer) and a *context data product* (meaning packaged in machine-readable form) and lays out the five-layer anatomy — base data product, ontology, semantic layer, knowledge graph, usage intelligence — plus exposure via a semantic API and a context-extended contract. Chapter 16 is the operational spine: a ninety-day, three-failure-mode-proof plan run day-by-day on Meridian's suitability domain. Chapter 17 goes deep on the craft — distinguishing ontology (the model of the domain), semantic layer (governed concept-to-calculation mapping), and knowledge graph (the populated instance) — with FIBO as accelerator, not project, and flags the operating-model dependency that Part V confronts.

**Continuity.** The Okonkwo/EM-frontier ambiguity from Chapter 5 is the exact terminology dispute the ontology resolves; the playbook's worked example (Client, Portfolio, Holding, Asset Class, Risk Profile) *is* Meridian's domain, so case study and method fuse cleanly. Maya owns the domain; the adviser copilot is the pulling consumer; the knowledge graph is where Parts II–IV visibly converge — capsule bindings, active-metadata trust signals, and ontology meaning in one place.

**Handoff.** Part IV is the complete *design* answer to "what does AI need from data?" — context bound, active, and packaged. Part V is the *assurance* answer: certification.
