# Part V — Prove

*The fourth move: turn packaged context into evidence a data product can produce about itself — on demand, for any consumer, human, machine, or regulator.*

Parts II–IV bound, activated, and packaged context. Part V is where a designed context data product becomes a *trusted* one: assessed, certified, and defensible under audit. It delivers the book's core promise — surviving the glass-walled room — and it lands the vignettes seeded earlier (Maya, Tom, Helena, Nadia).

**The arc.** Ch 17 develops Chapter 4's thesis into a working diagnostic — *AI-readiness is an evidence problem* — and names the six surfaces (ownership, metadata, quality, lineage, privacy, consumption), showing they are the capsule/active-metadata/context context asked to *testify*. Ch 18 turns evidence into assurance: readiness is conditional (ready for which use), assessed across five dimensions on a 0–5 level model, derived-not-authored, living in the product, queryable by agents, reassessed on change. Ch 19 is the field chapter — the six surfaces in three pairs (defend / govern / control) through Tom's silent quality programme, Nadia's claim-vs-receipt review, the churn-model ownership chain, and Maya's privacy question. Ch 20 confronts the operating-model dependency: mesh stalls (82%) and platform teams calcify for the same reason — no funded product ownership — and Maya-as-data-product-manager is why Meridian's discipline holds. Ch 21 answers the ROI question and separates the two disciplines. The Epilogue closes the loop: AI shifts from the consumer we ready data *for* to the producer that builds data itself, and the context discipline is its precondition.

**Vignette payoffs.** Maya (client-domain owner, model-risk question), Tom (data-quality programme), Helena (catalogue), and Nadia (model validation) — introduced across Parts II–III — land their full stories here, mapped to specific evidence surfaces. The Ch 7 model-risk moment is *recalled*, not re-told, so Part II's version still stands.

**Continuity.** Bind→Automate→Package→Prove completes; the certification 0–5 model is distinguished from the book's five-rung maturity ladder (finer-grained, per-product/per-use). The EU AI Act 2026 deadline from Chapter 4 returns as the regulatory clock behind Ch 19–20.
