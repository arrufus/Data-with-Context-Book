# Part V — Prove

*The fourth move: turn packaged context into evidence a data product can produce about itself — on demand, for any consumer, human, machine, or regulator.*

Parts II–IV bound, activated, and packaged context. Part V is where a *designed* context data product becomes a *trusted* one: assessed, certified, and defensible under audit. It delivers the book's core promise — surviving the glass-walled room — and lands the vignettes seeded earlier (Maya, Tom, Helena, Nadia), moving the product from Level 3 toward Level 4.

**The arc.** Chapter 18 develops Chapter 4's thesis into a working diagnostic — *AI-readiness is an evidence problem* — and names the six surfaces (ownership, metadata, quality, lineage, privacy, consumption), showing they are the capsule, active metadata, and context product asked to *testify*. Chapter 19 turns evidence into assurance: readiness is conditional (ready for which use), assessed across five dimensions on a 0–5 model, derived not authored, living in the product, queryable by agents, reassessed on change. Chapter 20 is the field chapter — the six surfaces in three pairs (defend / govern / control) through Tom's silent quality programme, Nadia's claim-vs-receipt review, the churn-model ownership chain, and Maya's privacy question. Chapter 21 confronts the operating-model dependency: data mesh stalls and platform teams calcify for the same reason — no funded product ownership — and Maya-as-data-product-manager is why Meridian's discipline holds. Chapter 22 answers the ROI question and separates the two disciplines. The Epilogue closes the loop: AI shifts from the consumer we ready data *for* to the producer that builds data itself, and the context discipline is its precondition.

**Vignette payoffs.** Maya (client-domain owner, model-risk question), Tom (data-quality programme), Helena (catalogue), and Nadia (model validation) — introduced across Parts II–III — land their full stories here, each mapped to a specific evidence surface. The Chapter 7 model-risk moment is *recalled*, not re-told, so Part II's version still stands.

**Continuity.** Bind → Automate → Package → Prove completes; the certification 0–5 model is kept distinct from the book's five-rung maturity ladder (it is finer-grained, per-product and per-use). The EU AI Act 2026 deadline from Chapter 4 returns as the regulatory clock behind Chapters 20–21.
